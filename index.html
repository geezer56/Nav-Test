World War 2 was a significant conflict in that it marked the first time that military operations on the ground were decisively influenced by the ability of an adversary to effectively control the airspace above the battlefield. In addition the battle for control of an adversary's airspace well behind the front lines of the ground conflict became of primary concern as the war progressed. These imperatives drove the rapid acceleration of aerial combat tactics, innovation in technology, and aircraft specialization that are evident in combat air wings today.
